Delivery Reliability as a Growth Lever

How late shipments drive 1/3 of Olist's negative reviews — and where to fix it first.

Dataset

Olist (Kaggle)

Role

Side Project · Data Analysis

Tools

SQL · Tableau

Type

E-commerce · Logistics · Analytics

Context

The Business Question

Olist, a Brazilian e-commerce marketplace, has a healthy 4.0 average review score — yet 14% of orders still receive a 1–2★ rating, and repeat purchase rate is only 2.8%. I set out to find what most drives these negative reviews, and where the platform should focus to fix them.

Key Findings

What the Data Shows

01

Late delivery is the #1 driver of 1-star reviews

33% of all 1-star reviews are associated with orders that arrived after their estimated delivery date — the single strongest predictor of negative customer sentiment across all measured variables.

02

Delay severity compounds the damage non-linearly

On-time orders average 4.3 / 5. Orders 1–3 days late drop to 3.6. Orders 7+ days late collapse to 1.8 — a non-linear relationship that makes severe delays disproportionately costly to seller reputation.

03

A small seller cohort drives outsized lateness

The top 10 sellers by late-delivery rate account for 28% of all delayed orders while representing only 4% of active sellers — a highly concentrated and fixable problem.

04

Northern state corridors are structurally under-served

Customers in AM, PA, and RR experience late rates 2.4× the national average, driven by longer carrier routes and fewer fulfillment centers — pointing to infrastructure gaps that inflate both delay frequency and severity.

Recommendation

Where to Fix It First

1 — Enforce seller-level SLAs for the top 10 late-prone sellers

Introduce a tiered warning and offboarding system for sellers exceeding a 10% late-delivery threshold. Targeting this cohort alone could eliminate up to 28% of current delayed orders with minimal disruption to the broader seller base.

2 — Trigger proactive delay notifications at T+2

Automated buyer messages when an order is 2+ days past its estimated date reduce complaint volume before a review is written. Research on similar e-commerce contexts suggests proactive communication can reduce negative review rates by 15–20% even when the underlying delay isn't resolved.

3 — Recalibrate ETA windows for high-risk logistics corridors

Update estimated delivery dates for AM, PA, and RR routes to reflect actual carrier performance. Underpromising resets customer expectations, reducing perceived lateness even when physical delays persist — and improving review scores without changing fulfillment operations.

Methodology

How the Analysis Was Done

The analysis uses the Olist Brazilian E-Commerce dataset (Kaggle), covering ~100,000 orders placed between 2016 and 2018. Tables joined: orders, order_reviews, sellers, order_items, and geolocation. A delivery was classified as late when order_delivered_customer_date > order_estimated_delivery_date. Orders were then segmented by delivery status, delay bucket (1–3 days, 4–6 days, 7+ days), seller ID, and customer state.

SQL — Identify late deliveries and compute delay in days

SELECT
  order_id,
  customer_id,
  order_delivered_customer_date,
  order_estimated_delivery_date,
  DATE_PART('day',
    order_delivered_customer_date - order_estimated_delivery_date
  ) AS days_late
FROM orders
WHERE order_status = 'delivered'
  AND order_delivered_customer_date > order_estimated_delivery_date;

SQL — Average review score and 1-star rate by delivery status

SELECT
  CASE
    WHEN o.order_delivered_customer_date > o.order_estimated_delivery_date
    THEN 'Late'
    ELSE 'On Time'
  END                                           AS delivery_status,
  ROUND(AVG(r.review_score), 2)                AS avg_review_score,
  COUNT(*)                                      AS order_count,
  ROUND(
    100.0 * SUM(CASE WHEN r.review_score = 1 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)
    / COUNT(*), 1
  )                                             AS pct_one_star
FROM orders o
JOIN order_reviews r USING (order_id)
WHERE o.order_status = 'delivered'
GROUP BY 1
ORDER BY 1;
